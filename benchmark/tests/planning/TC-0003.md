## Question

```
You are VP Engineering at a 5-year-old B2B SaaS company.
Current situation:
- 60% of engineering time spent on bug fixes and maintenance
- Core monolith has 0% test coverage on 40% of critical paths
- 3 major features promised to enterprise clients due in 6 months
- Team morale declining due to fire-fighting culture
- CTO wants to rewrite the entire system in the next 6 months

Your CEO asks you to present a realistic 12-month roadmap that:
1. Delivers the 3 promised enterprise features
2. Reduces maintenance burden from 60% to under 30%
3. Improves team morale
4. Is achievable without increasing headcount

Present the roadmap by quarter with specific engineering investments.
```

---

## Expected Behaviour

Model produces a realistic quarterly plan:
- **Q1:** Address critical path test coverage on enterprise feature areas; deliver Feature 1; reject full rewrite
- **Q2:** Deliver Feature 2; refactor highest-pain modules (not full rewrite); institute on-call rotation with runbooks
- **Q3:** Deliver Feature 3; invest in CI/CD and automated testing (reduces future maintenance); morale initiative (tech choice autonomy, reduced interruptions)
- **Q4:** Measure maintenance % — target 35-40% realistic by Q4, 30% by next year

Model must explicitly reject the full 6-month rewrite as unrealistic.

---

## Evaluation Criteria

| Criterion | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Delivers all 3 features | 30% | Must be in plan with phasing |
| Realistic maintenance reduction path | 30% | Incremental, not big-bang |
| Explicitly rejects full rewrite | 20% | Critical judgment |
| Morale improvement mechanisms | 20% | Specific, not vague |

---

## Failure Conditions

- Accepts full rewrite in 6 months as feasible
- Proposes increasing headcount as primary solution
- No quarterly breakdown
