## Question

```
Design a distributed event pipeline that must:
- Ingest 100,000 events/second at peak
- Guarantee at-least-once delivery
- P99 end-to-end latency < 500ms
- 99.99% availability (< 52 minutes downtime/year)
- Events must be replayable up to 7 days
- Support 50 downstream consumers with independent offsets
- Operate across 3 availability zones

Deliver:
1. Component diagram (described in text/ASCII)
2. Technology choices with justification
3. How you achieve 99.99% availability (specific mechanisms)
4. How you handle a single AZ failure (exact behaviour)
5. Capacity estimates for storage (7-day retention)
6. The single biggest risk in this design and its mitigation
```

---

## Expected Behaviour

1. **Ingestion layer**: Load balancer → Kafka (3 brokers, replication factor 3, min ISR 2) or AWS Kinesis with multi-AZ
2. **Technology**: Kafka is canonical answer; Kinesis, Pulsar, or Redpanda are acceptable with justification
3. **99.99% HA mechanisms**: Multi-AZ brokers, controller failover (<30s), consumer group rebalance, idempotent producers
4. **AZ failure**: Remaining 2 AZs absorb traffic; leader election within 30s; at-least-once via producer acks=all
5. **Storage estimate**: 100K events/s × avg 1KB = 100MB/s × 86,400s × 7 days = ~60TB raw; with RF3 = ~180TB. Model should arrive at same order of magnitude.
6. **Biggest risk**: Consumer lag amplification or hot partition → mitigation: partition key design, consumer scaling policy

## Failure Conditions

- Recommends a single-broker setup
- Claims 99.99% without specifying actual HA mechanisms
- Storage estimate off by >10x
- Does not address independent consumer offsets
