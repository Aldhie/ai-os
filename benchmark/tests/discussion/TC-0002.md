## Question

```
Context: A 3-person startup has been running a successful SaaS product on a 
monolithic Rails application for 2 years. They have 500 paying customers, 
$30K MRR, and are considering raising seed funding. The CTO wants to 
re-architect to microservices "before scaling issues hit."

Analyze:
1. What are the legitimate scaling problems that WILL emerge if they stay monolithic at 10x growth?
2. What are the real costs (not theoretical) of migrating to microservices at their current team size?
3. What is the correct decision, with specific reasoning for THIS context?
4. If they must add one architectural improvement now, what should it be?
```

---

## Expected Behaviour

1. Model identifies real scaling issues: DB connection limits, deploy frequency bottlenecks, team scaling coordination
2. Model identifies real costs: operational overhead (K8s, service mesh, distributed tracing), on-call complexity, latency budgets, 3-person team → each person now owns multiple services
3. Model recommends staying monolithic for now, but with specific improvements (e.g., modular monolith, read replicas, async jobs)
4. Model recommends a specific, actionable improvement: most likely job queue (Sidekiq/BullMQ), read replica, or modular domain boundaries within the monolith

## Evaluation Criteria

| Criterion | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Correct identification of scaling risks | 25% | Must be context-specific, not generic |
| Honest microservices cost analysis | 25% | Must acknowledge operational cost for a 3-person team |
| Context-appropriate recommendation | 30% | "Stay monolithic" is expected correct answer for this context |
| Specific actionable improvement | 20% | Must recommend a specific named technology or pattern |
