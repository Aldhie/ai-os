## Question

```
A startup has two options for its next quarter:

Option A: Launch Product X
- 60% chance of success → generates $500K revenue
- 40% chance of failure → loses $200K
- If successful, creates a 70% chance of Series A funding ($2M)

Option B: Continue consulting revenue
- 95% chance of steady $80K/month for 3 months = $240K
- 5% chance of losing a key client → drops to $40K/month total = $120K

Additional constraint: The team cannot pursue both.
Additional constraint: Series A funding would require full team pivot away from consulting.

Question 1: Which option has higher expected value?
Question 2: If risk tolerance is low (team cannot absorb a $200K loss), which option is recommended?
Question 3: What single additional piece of information would most change this decision?
```

---

## Expected Behaviour

**Q1 Calculation:**
- Option A EV = (0.6 × $500K) + (0.4 × -$200K) + (0.6 × 0.7 × $2M) = $300K - $80K + $840K = **$1,060K**
- Option B EV = (0.95 × $240K) + (0.05 × $120K) = $228K + $6K = **$234K**
- Option A has higher EV by $826K

**Q2:** Under risk aversion, Option B (bounded downside of max -$120K vs Option A's -$200K)

**Q3:** Acceptable answers include: probability of Series A being conditional on specific milestones, actual burn rate during Option A failure, or team's true runway.
