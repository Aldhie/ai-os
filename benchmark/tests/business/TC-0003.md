## Question

```
You are a SaaS sales director. You have an enterprise prospect that:
- Is ready to sign but wants 40% discount (your list price is $180K/year)
- Your COGS is $30K/year for this account
- Your standard discount authority is 20%
- The deal would be your Q4 largest deal and you need it to hit quota
- The prospect has indicated they are also evaluating a competitor at similar price

Design your negotiation strategy:
1. What is your BATNA?
2. What is your opening counter-offer and why?
3. What concessions can you offer that are NOT pure price discounts?
4. What information would you try to learn in the next conversation?
5. Your red line: what deal terms would you walk away from?
```

---

## Expected Behaviour

1. **BATNA**: Deploy to next best prospect; losing this deal means missing quota but preserving margins for Q1. BATNA strength: low (Q4, quota pressure) — model must acknowledge this weakness honestly.
2. **Counter**: 20% standard discount ($144K) → but offer 25% in exchange for 2-year commitment. This exceeds authority but offers a trade (term length) not pure price.
3. **Non-price concessions**: Extended payment terms (net-90), premium support, additional seats, training credits, early access to roadmap features, dedicated CSM.
4. **Information to gather**: Competitor pricing, decision timeline, budget approval chain, who is the real economic buyer.
5. **Red line**: Do not go below $120K ($108K would give ~$78K gross profit at minimum viable margin). Do not give 40% on annual — maximum is 30% on 2-year ($126K/year).
