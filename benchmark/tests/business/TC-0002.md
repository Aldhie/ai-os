## Question

```
A US-based B2B SaaS company (HR software, $5M ARR) wants to expand to SEA.
They have:
- $2M budget for market entry
- 18 months to reach profitability in the new market
- Product is English-only
- No existing SEA customers or partnerships
- Competitors in SEA: 2 established local players + Workday/SAP at enterprise tier

Provide:
1. Which country to enter first (justify with specific data points)
2. Entry mode (direct sales, partnerships, resellers, or PLG)
3. How to handle the English-only product constraint
4. Realistic 18-month revenue target with assumptions
5. The single highest risk and how to mitigate it
```

---

## Expected Behaviour

1. **Country**: Singapore first — English-primary business language, high GDP/capita, strong SME density, gateway reputation, regulatory clarity. Philippines or Indonesia as alternative with justification.
2. **Entry mode**: Channel partnerships or resellers for speed within $2M budget — direct enterprise sales is too expensive for this budget in a new market.
3. **English constraint**: Singapore acceptable; for Indonesia/Philippines, must have localization plan. AI should flag this as a real risk not just a note.
4. **Revenue target**: Conservative — $200K-$500K ARR in 18 months is realistic for Singapore SME market from cold start with $2M budget.
5. **Highest risk**: Product-market fit mismatch — SEA HR practices differ (e.g., THR payments in Indonesia, CPF in Singapore). Mitigation: discovery interviews before launch, local advisory board.
