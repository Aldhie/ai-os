## Question

```
Analyze the unit economics of this SaaS business and give a go/no-go recommendation:

- Monthly subscription: $99/user
- Average users per account: 5
- Monthly churn rate: 3.5%
- Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC): $1,800
- Cost to Serve (per account/month): $45
- Sales cycle: 45 days
- Current ARR: $2.4M
- MoM growth: 8%

Calculate:
1. Average Customer Lifetime (months)
2. LTV (Lifetime Value per account)
3. LTV:CAC ratio
4. CAC Payback Period (months)
5. Contribution Margin per account per month
6. Is this business viable? What is the single most important metric to improve?
```

---

## Expected Behaviour

1. **Avg Lifetime** = 1 / churn rate = 1 / 0.035 = **28.6 months**
2. **MRR per account** = $99 × 5 = $495/month
3. **LTV** = ($495 - $45) × 28.6 = $450 × 28.6 = **$12,870**
4. **LTV:CAC** = $12,870 / $1,800 = **7.15x** (above 3x threshold = healthy)
5. **CAC Payback** = $1,800 / $450 = **4 months** (excellent; <12 months is good)
6. **Contribution Margin** = ($495 - $45) / $495 = **90.9%**
7. **Viability**: Healthy unit economics. Single most important metric to improve: **churn rate** — dropping from 3.5% to 2% would increase LTV to $22,500, LTV:CAC to 12.5x.

## Evaluation Criteria

| Criterion | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Correct LTV calculation | 30% | Must use (MRR - CTS) × lifetime |
| Correct LTV:CAC | 20% | ~7x |
| Correct CAC payback | 20% | 4 months |
| Viability judgment | 15% | Clearly positive with justification |
| Churn identified as key lever | 15% | Specific reasoning |

---

## Failure Conditions

- Uses gross MRR (not net) for LTV
- LTV:CAC < 1 (arithmetic error)
- Cannot identify the most impactful lever
